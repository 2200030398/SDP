Gir Cow

The Gir cow, also known as the Bhadawari, Desan, Gujarati, Kathiawari, and Sorthi, is a famous breed of cow native to the Gir forest region of India and Pakistan. It is known for its distinctive appearance and high milk-producing capabilities.

The Gir cow is recognized by its characteristic hump on the shoulders and its long ears. It has a predominantly reddish-brown coat with shades varying from light to dark. This breed is highly prized for its tolerance to hot temperatures and resistance to various diseases.

Gir cows are primarily used for dairy purposes due to their high milk yield, which contains a high percentage of fat. They are also known for their docile temperament, making them easier to handle and manage compared to some other breeds.

The conservation of the Gir cow has been a focus of various initiatives in India to preserve its genetic purity and ensure its continued existence for future generations.
